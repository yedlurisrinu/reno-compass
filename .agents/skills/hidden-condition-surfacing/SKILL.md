# Hidden-Condition Surfacing

Raise likely-but-unseen conditions proportionately, as likelihoods not certainties (SI-2).

## How to reason
- Weight by `home_age` + `property_context`: older home → knob-and-tube wiring, no waterproofing membrane,
  undersized drains, lead paint (pre-1978), asbestos (pre-1980s).
- Keep proportionate — name the condition and why it's likely; never imply false precision.
- Home-age weighting produces the ballpark CONTINGENCY band (its own line, never folded into base; regionally
  scaled, base 10% × regional factor, clamped 20%).
- SCOPE-EXPANDING permit triggers: where a jurisdiction pulls work beyond the room, surface as a
  hidden-condition-class disclosure — e.g. CA SB 407 (a permit can require house-wide low-flow fixture
  replacement). AHJ-verify; gate by state so non-applicable regions don't inherit it.

## Boundaries
- Disclosures inform judgment; lead/asbestos are educational, NOT auto-escalations to Tier-1 (constitution
  Principle 4). Testing/abatement is the family's informed choice.
