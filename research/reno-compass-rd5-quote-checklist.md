## RD5-A. REQUIRED COVERAGE — what a complete quote MUST itemize  [the audit rubric]

**Textbook: a professional quote is a binding, itemized scope of work — not a lump-sum ballpark.** A bid that
reads "bathroom renovation — $X" with no breakdown is non-auditable and a red flag in itself. Coverage check
verifies each present/absent:

- RD5-A1. **Demolition + debris removal/haul** — down to studs/slab as needed; disposal explicitly priced
  (common silent omission).
- RD5-A2. **Plumbing — labor AND materials, listed separately** (rough-in, relocation, re-pipe to modern std).
- RD5-A3. **Electrical — labor AND materials, separately** (GFCI, circuits, fan, lighting; CA moisture-rated).
- RD5-A4. **Waterproofing membrane + backer/cement board** over all wet surfaces BEFORE tile — named system
  (e.g. sheet membrane / liquid-applied) and standard (e.g. ANSI A118.10 / shower-pan). **The single most
  common corner cut** (RD1-E); its absence is high-severity.
- RD5-A5. **Tile — materials + installation**, with the niche/bench/edge detail specified (not just "tile
  labor"). Note large-format (≥24 inch) subfloor-flatness prep if applicable.
- RD5-A6. **Each fixture with specific model # + price** (vanity, toilet, tub/shower, sink, faucet). Model
  numbers prevent bait-and-switch to lower grade.
- RD5-A7. **Permit line — building/plumbing/electrical/mechanical as applicable** (RD1-G). Explicit fee.
  **A contractor suggesting skipping permits is a major red flag** (title problem at resale).
- RD5-A8. **Labor hours or rates by trade** — lets the family sanity-check against RD-2/RD-3 rates.
- RD5-A9. **Project timeline with milestones** (start + estimated completion; which phases need inspection).
- RD5-A10. **Payment schedule tied to completion milestones** (NOT large upfront / full-before-start).
- RD5-A11. **Warranty terms** — labor/craftsmanship AND materials; who administers (in-house vs manufacturer).
- RD5-A12. **Change-order procedure** — written documenting/pricing/approval process before work proceeds.
- RD5-A13. **License + insurance/bond** — named licensed trades; verify with state board (e.g. CA CSLB).
  We do NOT validate license NUMBERS (CL-20) — we flag presence/absence + say "verify at [state board]."

## RD5-B. CORNER-CUTTING FLAGS — patterns that signal risk  [→ corner_cutting_flags, severity-tagged]

- RD5-B1. **HIGH — missing waterproofing line** (RD5-A4): leads to in-wall failure + shower rebuild.
- RD5-B2. **HIGH — no permit line where scope needs one** (relocation/wet-area/structural, RD1-G): unpermitted
  work = resale/title/insurance exposure.
- RD5-B3. **HIGH — missing licensed trade** for Tier-1/2 work (electrical panel, gas, structural): folds into
  corner_cutting_flags (CL-20; ex-tier_crossing).
- RD5-B4. **MEDIUM — suspiciously low bid / labor** vs RD-2/RD-3 bands: often hides omitted scope or grade
  downgrade. "Best value ≠ lowest price."
- RD5-B5. **MEDIUM — lump-sum, no itemization** (RD5-A): non-auditable; no accountability basis.
- RD5-B6. **MEDIUM — "allowance" placeholders** with no basis/cap: balloon at final selection (ties SI-16 —
  our allowances always carry a stated basis).
- RD5-B7. **MEDIUM — demolition/haul omitted** (RD5-A1): reappears as a change order.
- RD5-B8. **LOW–MEDIUM — no change-order procedure / no written warranty / large upfront payment.**
- RD5-B9. **LOW — no timeline or inspection milestones** (RD5-A9): schedule opacity.

## RD5-C. ADVISORY "WHAT TO DEMAND" — always generated, quote or not  [SI-25]

Even with no quote, the family gets a carry-in checklist: insist on itemized bid covering RD5-A1..A13; get
3–5 bids on IDENTICAL scope (apples-to-apples); verify license/insurance at the state board; confirm the
"invisible" inclusions (rough-in, waterproofing, permit, final cleanup) explicitly; hold final payment until
defects fixed and inspections signed off. *Industry stat (both-perspectives framing): ~60% of US renovations
go over budget, typically 15–30% — the checklist is the counter-move, paired with the RD2-E contingency.*

## RD5-D. TIMELINE CONTEXT  [industry-average + best-case, per prefs]

- RD5-D1. Permitting adds **industry-average 1–3 weeks** (to 3–5 in strict metros); **best-case ~1 week**
  simple scope. Custom vanities / specialty tile / frameless glass carry **2–4 week lead times**.
- RD5-D2. **Ordering all materials before demo is the single most effective on-schedule move** — a quote/
  contractor that can't give a written schedule with inspection milestones is itself a flag (RD5-B9).
